Build a complete production-ready India-first packaged food intelligence app called NutriScan AI.

The app helps users scan packaged food products, identify them through barcode first, use OCR only as fallback, analyze nutrition and ingredients, rate the product out of 10, personalize warnings based on the user’s health profile, store history, support manual product creation, and allow the owner/admin to manage the entire product catalog through a separate admin panel.

This is not a chat app.
Do not build a standalone AI assistant or chat screen.
AI is only allowed as a structured explanation layer inside product analysis, ingredient summaries, verdict generation, and personalization wording.

The app must feel fast, trustworthy, practical, and useful for Indian users.
It must be cheap to run, easy to maintain, and built in a way that lets the owner manually add or correct products when public APIs do not have them.

PRIMARY GOAL
A user should be able to:
1. open the app,
2. scan a barcode or search a product,
3. instantly understand what the product is,
4. see a health score out of 10,
5. see why the score is what it is,
6. see whether the product fits their health profile,
7. save it to history or pantry,
8. and continue even if the public barcode API has no record.

INDIA-FIRST PRODUCT DIRECTION
- Build for India first.
- Use Indian food habits, Indian packaged food brands, Indian labels, Indian serving sizes, Indian price sensitivity, and Indian internet conditions.
- Support English and Hindi at minimum.
- Use INR pricing in the monetization UI.
- Make the app work on low-end Android phones.
- Keep the app usable on weak or unstable internet.
- Cache repeated results aggressively.
- Support vegetarian, vegan, Jain-friendly, halal-aware, allergen-aware, low-sugar, and low-sodium use cases.
- Prefer plain language over technical jargon in user-facing text.
- Show Indian product examples in discover, onboarding, empty states, and search suggestions.

NO STANDALONE AI CHAT
- Do not create any screen, feature, or menu item called AI Chat, Ask AI, Chat Assistant, or similar.
- Do not provide free-form conversational chat.
- Do not create a multi-turn chat experience.
- Replace chat with structured “Why this score?”, “Ingredient explanation”, “What to watch for”, and “Suitability for you” sections on the result screen.

SCORING SYSTEM: HEALTH SCORE OUT OF 10
The score must be transparent, explainable, and category aware.

The health score is a consumer guidance score, not a medical diagnosis.
It should tell the user if the product is generally better or worse relative to alternatives.

Use these scoring components:
- calories / energy density
- added sugar
- sodium
- saturated fat
- trans fat
- fiber
- protein quality
- ingredient quality
- additive risk
- processing level
- completeness of data
- personalization adjustment
- category-specific weighting

General formula:
finalScore = clamp(0, 10, baseNutritionScore + ingredientQualityScore + additiveScore + personalizationAdjustment - missingDataPenalty - ultraProcessedPenalty)

The result must always be rounded to one decimal place.

SCORING PRINCIPLES
- High sugar lowers the score heavily.
- Sodium lowers the score heavily in snacks, noodles, condiments, and drinks.
- Trans fat should be penalized aggressively.
- Saturated fat should be penalized moderately.
- Fiber should improve the score.
- Protein should improve the score, but must not hide poor ingredients.
- Very long ingredient lists or very artificial ingredient profiles should lower the score.
- Additives should be classified clearly.
- If the product data is incomplete, confidence should fall.
- If OCR data is uncertain, confidence must be shown as low or medium.
- The score should adapt to the product category.

- if fallback fails, use a deterministic rule-based explanation from the scoring engine

The AI output must be structured JSON.
